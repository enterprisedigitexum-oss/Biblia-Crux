# Biblia Crux

**Biblia Crux** is an open source project dedicated to building a multilingual Catholic Bible database, structured for use in websites, applications, editorial materials, evangelization projects, Bible study, catechesis, and spiritual formation.

The goal of the project is to provide a Catholic biblical database in an open format, initially organized in six languages:

- Portuguese
- English
- French
- Italian
- Spanish
- Tagalog / Filipino

These languages were chosen because of their great pastoral and demographic relevance within the Catholic Church. Together, they have the potential to reach approximately **70% to 80% of the world’s Catholics**, depending on the criterion used: native language, official language, institutional language, functional second language, or pastoral and digital use.

Biblia Crux was born as a rare and pioneering initiative: an attempt to create an open, reusable, multilingual Catholic Bible database capable of serving not only one specific application, but also many other Catholic projects in the future.

## Why does this project exist?

Today, there are many digital biblical resources, but it is still difficult to find a **Catholic**, **multilingual**, **structured**, **open** database designed from the beginning to be reused by other projects.

Biblia Crux seeks to fill this gap.

This project was created to help make the Word of God more accessible in different languages, especially for Catholic communities around the world. The proposal is to build a database that can be reused by:

- Catholic websites
- Bible applications
- Catechesis projects
- Spiritual formation materials
- Bible study tools
- Pastoral and missionary projects
- Educational and editorial initiatives

Improvements made here can benefit many other projects, expanding access to Sacred Scripture in different cultural and linguistic contexts.

## Origin of the texts and methodology

The multilingual biblical texts of Biblia Crux were structured with the support of Artificial Intelligence and editorial review, using as their main basis:

- **Douay-Rheims Bible**, in English
- **Clementina Vulgata**, in Latin
- **Catholic Public Domain Version (CPDV)**

These references were chosen because they are in the public domain or because they allow the creation of a textual base compatible with an open and reusable proposal.

From these sources, Biblia Crux seeks to offer a consistent database in six languages:

- Portuguese
- English
- French
- Italian
- Spanish
- Tagalog / Filipino

This multilingual database was designed not only for the current application, but also to support new initiatives in the future, including other applications, editorial materials, and projects focused on evangelization, Bible study, and spiritual formation.

---

## Use of Artificial Intelligence

The translations and textual structures of Biblia Crux were generated and organized with the assistance of Artificial Intelligence.

For this reason, the project needs the collaboration of the community for review, correction, and continuous improvement.

Even with editorial care, there may be cases where a translation was not rendered in the best possible way, for example:

- Proper names translated incorrectly
- Biblical terms that could be better adapted
- Expressions that need greater clarity
- Sentences that need grammatical revision
- Words that require greater doctrinal or pastoral precision
- Cultural differences between languages

Biblia Crux is therefore a growing database. Its quality depends on the responsible collaboration of people committed to fidelity to the Word of God and to Catholic tradition.

## Linguistic reach

The choice of the six initial languages has a pastoral and strategic motivation.

Portuguese, English, French, Italian, Spanish, and Tagalog/Filipino are connected to some of the largest Catholic populations in the world, including:

- Latin America
- Brazil
- United States
- Philippines
- Western Europe
- Important parts of Africa
- Catholic migrant communities on several continents

With these six languages, Biblia Crux is estimated to be able to reach a very significant portion of the global Catholic population:

| Coverage criterion | Approximate estimate |
|---|---:|
| Native or dominant language | Around 70% of Catholics |
| Official, national, or institutional language | Around 75% of Catholics |
| Functional second language, pastoral, or digital use | Up to around 80% of Catholics |

In absolute numbers, this represents approximately between **995 million and 1.14 billion Catholics** potentially reached.

These values are estimates and should be understood as a pastoral and demographic approximation, not as an exact count. Even so, they show the strategic importance of these six languages for international biblical and Catholic projects.

## An open database for the Catholic community

Biblia Crux is not intended to be merely a collection of files.

It is intended to be an open database so that the Catholic community can build, review, improve, and reuse biblical texts in multiple languages.

This project begins from a simple conviction:

> The Word of God should become increasingly accessible, understandable, and available to all peoples.

For this reason, every correction submitted, every suggestion reviewed, and every improvement applied can help not only this repository, but also other Catholic projects that may use this database in the future.

## How to contribute

You can contribute in two main ways.

### 1. Through the website form

The simplest way is to submit a suggestion through the form:

https://enterprisedigitexum-oss.github.io/Biblia-Crux/bible-forms/

Use the form to indicate:

- Biblical book
- Chapter
- Verse
- Language
- Correction suggestion
- Comment explaining the change

### 2. Through GitHub

Contributors with technical knowledge can also suggest corrections directly through GitHub.

This option is recommended for developers or people who are already familiar with how GitHub works.

In this case, the contributor can follow these steps:

1. Fork the repository
2. Create a branch for the change
3. Edit the corresponding translation file inside the `bible-translations` folder
4. Carefully review the change
5. Open a Pull Request explaining the proposed correction

The Pull Request will be reviewed before being incorporated into the main repository.

If you do not have experience with GitHub, we recommend using the website form:

https://enterprisedigitexum-oss.github.io/Biblia-Crux/bible-forms/

---

## Important care when contributing

When suggesting a correction, please observe:

- Fidelity to the biblical meaning
- Respect for Catholic tradition
- Pastoral clarity
- Good grammar in the corresponding language
- Consistency with the rest of the translation
- Care with proper names, places, and theological terms
- Respect for the copyrights of other Bible translations
- Use of other Bibles only as study references, without copying protected texts in full

Contributions should seek to improve the quality of Biblia Crux responsibly, avoiding the simple reproduction of Bible translations protected by copyright.

Contributions should be made in a spirit of service, respect, and responsibility.
